# Content Strategy

## Purpose

This document defines the content structure, storytelling approach, user journey, messaging hierarchy, and content requirements for the Ignite The Soul website.

Rather than simply documenting pages, this guide explains how visitors should emotionally move through the website and what each section must communicate.

Every piece of content should support one goal:

**Help visitors feel safe, understood, and inspired before inviting them to take action.**

---

# Website Structure

The website consists of five primary pages.

```
/
Home

/about
About

/offerings
Offerings

/testimonials
Testimonials

/contact
Contact
```

The website intentionally keeps navigation simple to create a calm browsing experience.

---

# User Journey

The website is designed around emotion rather than information.

Visitors should naturally move through the following journey.

Discover

↓

Feel Welcome

↓

Understand The Mission

↓

Meet Mafaza

↓

Explore Experiences

↓

Build Trust

↓

Register Interest

The experience should never feel rushed.

---

# Content Philosophy

Visitors should feel like they are reading a beautifully designed wellness magazine.

Not a business brochure.

Content should:

- Feel conversational
- Feel personal
- Feel spacious
- Feel intentional
- Feel authentic

Never overwhelming.

## Offerings

Purpose

Present every experience in a calm, structured way.

Avoid overwhelming visitors with information.

### Experiences

Coffee Talks

Workshops

Group Coaching

Soulful Farm Retreat

Each of these 4 Experiences is evergreen and can highlight one or more
specific, time-bound Events underneath it (e.g. Soulful Farm Retreat → the
October 2026 retreat, Workshops → Art Therapy / Vision Board Workshop dates,
Group Coaching → an August cohort). Events come and go; the Experience
stays. An Experience with no active Event just shows its description.

The Offerings page keeps these two ideas visually separate: "Upcoming
Experiences" is the flat, date-led list of active Events; "Explore Our
Experiences" introduces the 4 Experiences themselves as alternating
image/text sections. Neither section mixes the two concepts.

Each highlighted Event should contain:

Title, status, and date/cadence

A short summary (only when real, verified copy exists — never invented)

Optionally: itinerary, what's included, investment, logistics (as available,
not fabricated when real details don't exist yet)

Registration Form (implemented as one shared Contact form with an offering
selector, pre-filled from the originating CTA, rather than a separate form
per experience)

# Storytelling Framework

Every page should follow the same storytelling pattern.

Emotion

↓

Understanding

↓

Trust

↓

Invitation

Never begin with detailed explanations.

Always begin with emotion.

---

# Writing Style

Use natural language.

Short paragraphs.

Comfortable reading rhythm.

Generous spacing.

Speak directly to the reader.

Avoid complicated language.

---

# Headlines

Headlines should inspire curiosity rather than explain everything.

Examples

Reconnect with Yourself

Begin Your Journey

You Don't Have To Walk Alone

Find Your Safe Space

Grow With Community

Choose warmth over cleverness.

---

# Body Copy

Body copy should answer questions naturally.

Instead of listing features:

Describe experiences.

Instead of selling:

Guide.

Instead of convincing:

Reassure.

---

# Calls to Action

The website should avoid aggressive marketing language.

Preferred CTAs

Begin Your Journey

Explore Our Experiences

Learn More

Register Your Interest

Join the Circle

Reach Out

Limited Time

Sign Up Today

Don't Miss Out
