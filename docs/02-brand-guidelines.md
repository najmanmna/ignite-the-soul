# Brand Guidelines

## Purpose

This document defines the identity, personality, voice, emotional direction, and visual language of Ignite The Soul.

It serves as the foundation for every creative decision made throughout the project—from copywriting and UI design to photography, animation, and user experience.

Every component should reinforce the same feeling:

**Calm. Warm. Safe. Intentional.**

# Brand Essence

Ignite The Soul exists to help women reconnect with themselves through meaningful experiences, supportive community, intentional reflection, and guided personal growth.

The brand is built on the belief that lasting transformation begins within.

Rather than offering quick solutions or motivational hype, Ignite The Soul creates safe spaces where women can slow down, feel supported, and rediscover themselves.

The website should communicate this before communicating any service.

---

# Brand Vision

To become Sri Lanka's most trusted women's wellness community where women feel seen, supported, empowered, and inspired to grow together.

---

# Brand Mission

To create meaningful experiences through coaching, retreats, workshops, and women's circles that encourage emotional wellbeing, resilience, confidence, and authentic personal growth.

# Tone of Voice

The voice should always feel conversational, warm, and reassuring.

Write as if Mafaza is personally welcoming someone into a peaceful retreat.

Use:

"We"

"You"

"Together"

"Our community"

"Your journey"

Avoid sounding like a sales page.

---

# Writing Style

Keep sentences natural.

Leave breathing room.

Avoid overly long paragraphs.

Use thoughtful spacing.

Write with empathy.

Never sound like marketing.

# Typography Direction

Headings

Elegant editorial serif.

Examples

- Cormorant Garamond
- Playfair Display
- Canela

Body

Modern sans-serif.

Examples

- Inter
- Manrope
- Lato

Typography should feel timeless rather than trendy.

---

# Photography Style

Use real photography whenever possible.

Preferred imagery includes:

- Women's circles
- Nature
- Journaling
- Conversations
- Reflection
- Workshops
- Retreat moments
- Tea
- Sunrise
- Soft natural lighting
- Genuine emotions

Avoid:

Corporate stock photography

Overly posed influencer images

Fake smiles

Artificial lighting

Visual clutter

---

# Layout Philosophy

Whitespace is a design element.

Every section should breathe.

Large margins.

Minimal distractions.

Strong typography.

Editorial composition.

Subtle motion.

Quality over quantity.

---

# Video Style

Videos should feel authentic and conversational.

Prefer:

- Natural lighting
- Genuine emotion
- Relaxed conversation
- Simple backgrounds
- Minimal editing

Avoid:

- Scripted marketing videos
- Heavy transitions
- Promotional music
- Over-produced visuals

# Motion & Animation

Motion should feel almost invisible.

Slow fades.

Gentle image movement.

Natural scrolling.

Soft transitions.

No bouncing.

No spinning.

No flashy effects.

Animations should support calmness, never distract from it.

---

# Brand Principles

Every design decision should satisfy these principles.

## Calm Before Conversion

Build trust before asking users to register.

---

## Story Before Services

Tell the brand story before presenting offerings.

---

## Emotion Before Information

Create connection before presenting details.

---

## Simplicity Before Complexity

If a feature doesn't improve the visitor's experience, remove it.

---

## Humanity Before Technology

The visitor should remember how the website made them feel, not which technologies were used.

---

# Brand Don'ts

Never create layouts that feel like templates.

Never use excessive gradients.

Never overuse decorative icons.

Never clutter pages.

Never use urgency marketing.

Never use aggressive calls to action.

Never sacrifice whitespace.

Never compromise accessibility for aesthetics.

Never make the website feel like a coaching sales funnel.
